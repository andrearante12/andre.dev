About

Who I am

I'm an undergraduate computer engineering student at George Washington University with a deep focus on robotics and computer vision. My work spans the full stack — from training neural networks to wiring servos and electrical components.

Currently, I work in the Intelligent Aerospace Systems Lab (IASL) at GWU, where my research focuses on open-vocabulary obstruction detection for drone package delivery using YOLO-World deployed on a Jetson Xavier. Previously, I have worked on autonomous vehicle simulations such as CARLA/SUMO simulators, integrated with OpenCDA at IASL, and full-stack web development and building autonomous UGV systems as an intern at C-3 Comm Systems LLC. Outside of work, I'm currently building: a 6-DOF robotic arm, RL agents in Minecraft, and custom smart home systems.
